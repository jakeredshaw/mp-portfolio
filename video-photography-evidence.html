Photography basics

For my photography portfolio I used a combination of my mobile phone and a Fujifilm X-T3 photography camera supplied by UA92. I wanted to use a variety of technology so I could capture different pictures in different situations, and utilize the automatic functions of the phone to control the ISO, shutter speed and focus, and then change to creating a personalized combination on the professional camera for personalized images. The theme for my portfolio is fashion and body language, because I’m very interested in the way people present themselves, and ow people perceive and interpret body languages, and therefor mixing different body languages with different styles of clothing, there could be a potential increase in sales for a company who utilizes this technique due to the target audience being more appealed by how the clothes are represented on the models body. There was strobe lighting and natural lighting used throughout the portfolio to exaggerate the different styles and poses used. Editing my pictures, I used Adobe Photoshop to colour, contrast and in some cases straighten the photo so the main image was in the perfect position. I also down sized the pictures using Photopea due to the web design requirements. ‘$tanding Proud’, my first photo I took from a 45degree angle to my model and then on my left used a strobe light directly facing him to open up his clothing and body language to the camera, then I used Photoshop to edit any background noise to black so my main image was the only thing being looked at. My second photo, ‘Flexin’, I took from front on and using a mid ISO and a fast shutter speed to make sure his dark clothing was shown and also so the image didn’t blur during his movement, I had to get the perfect mix of the two (Rachel, 2018). For ‘The Other $ide’, my third image, I increased contrast a lot due to the mirror surprising sapping out a lot of colour. I needed the colour in this image due to that radiant orange juxtaposed to the models antisocial body language. For the picture ‘Feelin Blue’ I tilted the camera low to the floor to maximize the main image size in the photo, and also so the picture wasn’t too wide, and therefor leaving a lot of background. Also, I feel this tilt I used gives the image movement and the low angle allows for not many shows to be shown therefor brightening the image with editing needed. Last but not least there is ‘Only Way I$ Up’, this is the only photo taken on an iPhone camera and I took it laying on the ground with the camera vertical, giving it the tall properties I wanted for the image. Rachel, G Medium. Dec, 4, 2018. Photography Basics — Shutter Speed and ISO. Video production basics

The group video production task was a VT insert for BBC’s Match of The Day X program. We filmed this insert in widescreen format due to it being on the TV, this meant the editing and framing had to be more formally approached. The video is around 2 minutes long due to it being a VT, and not wanting to take up too much time however still being full of energy and information. In the studio we used to film, we acted more on a personal level to camera due to the show having a casual atmosphere. We used 4 different locations for our video production, we filmed in the UA92 media lab, outside the university, inside the main entrance of the university and on the canal passing by the Old Trafford football ground for our interviews. For the sound, when outside we used a dead-cat on the boom mic to reduce wind and background noise, and when inside the studio, me and my co-host Kieran used small clip on mics. The editing was done on Adobe Premier Pro. In the studio, our lighting set up was 3 strobe lights pointing at the sofa, 1 from the left, 1 from the right, and one in the centre, a pink ceiling light above the sofa giving us small amounts of backlight but more importantly lighting up our background, this allowed for the hosts to be fully lit at all times, and is called Four-Point lighting (Ikan, 2020). During the interviews, an iPhone torch and natural lighting was utilised. Ikan. May, 27, 2020. Television Studio Lighting Techniques. [online] Available at: https://ikancorp.com/television-studio-lighting-techniques/ [Accessed 21 June 2021].

Social Media delivery formants

Deciding to create a small clip for multi social medias, the group decided to create a square format of the video fit all platforms therefore targeting the widest range of consumers. Instagram, Facebook, Twitter, Tiktok and snapchat are the platforms that we would potentially post this clip on. All thee social medias have easy interaction, so we would also be able to gather feedback very effectively too. The group decided to use the topic of Marcus Rashford MBE in our 22 second clip. The clip was compiled with different moments in the long cut that involved Rashford. Since Marcus became a ‘lockdown hero’ (Robinson, 2020) he has been one of the leading role models for the upcoming generation, and a credit to the young adults of todays generation, using Marcus’ name in social media posts will bring in views and if they like the content they will share it around therefor spreading our potential viewing number.
